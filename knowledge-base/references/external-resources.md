## Physical Modeling and Synthesis

### Books

- *Physical Audio Signal Processing* by Julius O. Smith III ([free online](https://ccrma.stanford.edu/~jos/pasp/))
- *The Computer Music Tutorial* by Curtis Roads (comprehensive DSP and synthesis)
- *Designing Sound* by Andy Farnell (procedural audio, physical modeling)

### Papers

- Karplus-Strong: "Digital Synthesis of Plucked-String and Drum Timbres" (1983)
- Jaffe & Smith: "Extensions of the Karplus-Strong Plucked-String Algorithm" (1983)
- Modal Synthesis: "Modal Synthesis: Casting Physical Modeling in a Sinusoidal Mold" (Jean-Marie Adrien)

### Tools and Examples

- [CCRMA (Stanford) - DSP and Physical Modeling Resources](https://ccrma.stanford.edu/)
- [Faust Language](https://faust.grame.fr/) (functional DSP; can export to Max/gen~)
- Fors Mass (modal percussion synth; reference for M4L implementation)

## Algorithmic Composition

### Books

- *The Algorithmic Beauty of Plants* by Prusinkiewicz & Lindenmayer (L-systems)
- *Formalized Music* by Iannis Xenakis (stochastic composition, granular synthesis)
- *Computer Models of Musical Creativity* by David Cope (algorithmic composition, style modeling)
- *META Meta-Hodos* by James Tenney (gestalt perception, form)

### Papers

- Steve Reich: "Music as a Gradual Process" (1968) — process music manifesto
- Tom Johnson: *Self-Similar Melodies* (algorithmic self-similarity)
- Various: Google Scholar for "algorithmic composition," "L-systems music," "generative music"

### Software

- [AlgoScore](https://www.algorithmiccomposer.com/) — notation + algorithmic rules
- [ORCA](https://hundredrabbits.itch.io/orca) — live-coding environment (esoteric but interesting)
- [Nodal Music](https://nodalmusic.com/) — graph-based generative sequencer
