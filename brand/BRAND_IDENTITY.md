# Stria Brand Identity

Single source of truth for the Stria brand. All products, communications, and marketing should align with this identity.

---

## 1. Brand Definition

**Stria** is a brand creating algorithmic music tools for artists who want **transparent, deterministic generative systems** — not AI black boxes.

### What We Make

Algorithmic music tools: sequencers, synthesizers, effects, and complete instruments that use explicit mathematical and compositional processes (phasing, L-systems, group theory, process music) rather than neural networks or opaque "AI."

### What We Stand For

| Value | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| **Artist-driven** | Created by a composer with a DMA; research-grade craft, not corporate product. |
| **Transparent logic** | Every note, every process is deterministic and understandable. You see the rules. |
| **Human-designed** | Generative without the black box. You stay in control. |
| **Process music heritage** | Rooted in Reich, Glass, Tenney, and algorithmic composition research. |

---

## 2. Positioning

### Primary Statement

**"Logic, not AI."**

Stria makes algorithmic music tools with transparent, deterministic rules — generative systems you understand and control.

### Secondary Positioning

- **Research-grade** — Built from DMA-level algorithmic composition study
- **Artist tools** — Not toys, not presets, not "AI-powered magic"
- **Process-based** — One clear process per tool: phasing, recursion, symmetry, division, etc.

### Against What?

- **Not generative AI** — No neural nets, no prompts, no training on copyrighted material
- **Not random** — Stochastic when useful, but always with visible parameters
- **Not feature-bloat** — Each tool does one thing deeply, not everything shallowly

## 3. Brand Hierarchy

```
Stria (brand)
├─ Drift (first product: phase sequencer + struck-bar synth)
├─ [Future single-word product names]
└─ Knowledge base, research, process
```

### Naming Rules

- **Brand name**: **Stria** (appears on product pages, website, social)
- **Product names**: Single evocative words (Drift, Fold, Shift, Loop)
- **Format**: "Drift by Stria" or "Stria — Drift"
- **Never**: "Stria Phasing Engine Pro" or descriptive compound names

---

## 4. Target Audience

### Primary

- **Electronic music producers** (Ableton, Max for Live, VST) who want evolving patterns without randomness or AI
- **Experimental / IDM / ambient artists** who value process and structure
- **Live performers** needing generative systems that stay musical and controllable

### Secondary

- **Composers** with algorithmic or process music background (DMA/PhD, research, academia)
- **Modular / hardware users** who appreciate transparent logic and determinism
- **Educators** teaching generative music, algorithmic composition

### Psychographics

- Values **craft** and **depth** over shortcuts
- Wants **control** and **understanding**, not "magic"
- Appreciates **music theory**, **math**, and **process** as creative tools
- Skeptical of "AI-powered" hype; prefers explicit systems

## 5. Differentiation (vs. Competitors)

| Competitor | What They Do | How Stria Differs |
|-----------|--------------|-------------------|
| **Fors** (Opal, Mass, etc.) | Boutique M4L: sequencers + synths, param locks, probability | We focus on **one algorithmic process per tool** (e.g. phase-based, L-system); clearer conceptual identity. |
| **Dillon Bastan** (Polychord, Particle Farm) | Generative MIDI: stochastic, physics-like, emergent | Our processes are **deterministic and music-theory-grounded** (Reich, Tenney, group theory), not physics simulation. |
| **Sugar Bytes** (Thesys, Consequence) | Step sequencers with mod and randomness | We're **process-first** (e.g. phasing, recursion) and position as research-grade, not feature-packed. |
| **Neural / AI tools** (AIVA, Magenta, etc.) | Black-box ML models, prompt-based | **"Logic, not AI."** Transparent rules; you see and control the process. |

### Our Unique Position

**Transparent algorithmic music tools with a clear conceptual and compositional lineage** — each product embodies one process (phasing, recursion, symmetry, etc.) from algorithmic composition research, not generic "generative" or random.

## 6. Brand Promise (One Sentence)

**Stria gives you research-grade algorithmic music tools—one clear process per instrument, transparent logic, no AI—so you stay in control and understand what you hear.**

## 7. Tone and Voice (High-Level)

See [VOICE_GUIDELINES.md](VOICE_GUIDELINES.md) for detailed examples. Quick summary:

- **Confident, not loud** — No "revolutionary" or "game-changing"
- **Clear** — Name the process (phase sequencer, L-system, struck bar); no vague "magic"
- **Warm** — Invite curiosity: "Hear what one idea becomes"
- **Expert** — OK to mention Reich, process music, algorithms; we're research-grade

---

## 8. Visual Identity (High-Level)

See [VISUAL_IDENTITY.md](VISUAL_IDENTITY.md) for details. Quick summary:

- **Minimal** — Plenty of space, limited palette, no clutter
- **Two-tone or limited color** — One accent color; clean typography
- **Designed, not toy-like** — Signals "pro tool" and craft
- **Fors/Elektron-inspired** — Clean, scalable UI; no clip art

## 9. Content Pillars

All marketing, docs, and communications should touch on these themes:

| Pillar | Explanation | Example |
|--------|-------------|---------|
| **The Process** | What algorithmic or compositional process this tool embodies | "Phase-based sequencing: two playheads drift over one pattern" |
| **The Control** | How you stay in charge; transparent, not AI | "You choose the pattern; we handle the phase" |
| **The Craft** | DMA-led, research-grade, process music heritage | "From Reich's phase pieces to your rack" |
| **The Experience** | What it feels like to use; invitation to explore | "Hear what one idea becomes as the playheads drift" |

---

## 10. Checklist: Does This Align with Stria?

Use this for any new product, copy, or design decision:

- [ ] Is the **process** clear and named? (Not vague "generative")
- [ ] Is it **deterministic or transparent**? (Not black-box AI)
- [ ] Does it fit **"Logic, not AI"**? (Explicit rules, not neural net)
- [ ] Is the **name** a single evocative word? (Not descriptive compound)
- [ ] Is the **voice** confident and clear, not hype? (No "revolutionary")
- [ ] Does it reference **craft or research** if appropriate? (DMA, process music, algorithm)
- [ ] Does the **visual** feel minimal and designed? (Not clip art or toy-like)
