# Voice Guidelines: Stria

Tone, messaging, and copy examples for all Stria communications. Use these guidelines for product pages, marketing, docs, social media, and any user-facing content.

---

## Voice Attributes

| Attribute | Meaning for Stria | What to Avoid |
|-----------|-------------------|---------------|
| **Clear** | Say what it is: "phase sequencer," "two playheads," "L-system." Name the process. | Vague "generative magic," "AI-powered creativity," "intelligent algorithms" |
| **Confident** | State value directly: "Research-grade phasing." "You choose the pattern; the engine does the rest." | Hype: "revolutionary," "game-changing," "ultimate," "industry-leading" |
| **Warm** | Invite curiosity and play: "Hear what one idea becomes." "Capture the moment." | Cold/clinical: "optimize workflow," "maximize efficiency," "leverage synergy" |
| **Expert** | Reference craft: Reich, process music, algorithms, DMA-led. Signals depth. | Jargon for its own sake; assume knowledge without explaining; gatekeeping |

## Core Messaging

### Tier 1: Headline / Hero (5–10 words)

Primary taglines and product type descriptors.

**Examples:**
- "One pattern. Two playheads. Endless drift."
- "Phase-based generative sequencing for Ableton."
- "Fractal composition from one seed."
- "Structure from symmetry."

**Pattern:** Short, clear, poetic. Type (sequencer, instrument) + core process (phase, L-system, tiling).

---

### Tier 2: Product Page / Short Copy (1–2 sentences)

Experiential description; what it feels like to use.

**Examples:**
- "Drift is a phase-based generative sequencer. You define a pattern; two playheads drift over it at slightly different rates. The result is endlessly shifting variations—deterministic, musical, and fully under your control. No AI, no black box: just transparent logic built for the studio."
- "One idea, grown by rule. L-system sequencing lets you define a seed and replacement rules; the device grows self-similar, branching patterns from that single phrase. Bach-like structure, algorithmic depth, no randomness."
- "Your motif becomes a crystal. Choose a symmetry group; the sequencer fills the grid with rotations, reflections, and translations of your pattern. Structure from math, music from geometry."

**Pattern:** State what it is → describe the experience → emphasize control/logic/transparency. No hype; focus on "what" and "how it feels."

---

### Tier 3: Long-Form / About / Education (2–3 paragraphs)

Deeper context; heritage, craft, positioning.

**Example (Drift):**

> Drift brings phase-based sequencing to Ableton. Inspired by Steve Reich's *Piano Phase* and process music, Drift lets you define a pattern and then hear it unfold through two independent playheads that drift apart over time. The result is musical variation from a single idea—no randomness, no AI, just transparent logic you can see and control.
> 
> Built by a composer with a DMA in algorithmic composition, Drift is research-grade: every note, every parameter, every process is deterministic and audible. You choose the pattern; the engine handles the phase. Capture moments as they emerge, quantize to any scale, and send MIDI to external instruments or use the built-in struck-bar physical model for a complete instrument.
> 
> From process music to your rack. One pattern, two playheads, endless drift.

**Pattern:** Context (what/why) → craft/credentials (DMA, research-grade) → invitation (how to use, what you get). Warm but expert; no hype.

---

### Tier 4: Differentiation ("Logic, not AI")

Positioning against competitors and generative AI.

**Examples:**
- "Logic, not AI."
- "Generative without the black box."
- "You stay in control."
- "Transparent rules, deterministic output."
- "Artist-driven algorithmic tools."
- "Human-designed, research-grade."

**Usage:** Tag line, footer, "About Stria" page, any comparison context (e.g. "unlike AI-powered tools…").

## Do's and Don'ts

### Do:

- **Name the process**: "phase sequencer," "L-system," "tiling," "struck-bar physical model"
- **Use numbers and specifics**: "two playheads," "2–4 partials," "recursion depth," "17-step loop"
- **Reference heritage**: "Inspired by Reich," "from process music," "DMA-led," "algorithmic composition"
- **Invite exploration**: "Hear what one idea becomes," "capture the moment," "let the structure unfold"
- **State control**: "You define the pattern," "transparent logic," "deterministic," "you stay in charge"

### Don't:

- **Hype words**: revolutionary, game-changing, ultimate, next-generation, cutting-edge, groundbreaking
- **AI buzzwords**: AI-powered, neural, intelligent, smart, learns, adapts (unless we literally mean machine learning, which we don't)
- **Vague generative**: "creates infinite possibilities," "intelligent algorithms," "magic under the hood"
- **Oversell**: "best," "only," "most powerful," "industry-leading"
- **Corporate speak**: "leverage," "synergy," "optimize workflow," "maximize efficiency," "end-to-end solution"

## Messaging by Audience

### For Producers (Ableton / DAW users)

Focus on **workflow, musicality, control**.

**Example:**
> Drift sits in your rack like any M4L device. Define a pattern, dial in drift and rate, and you're done. Capture phrases as they emerge; send MIDI to any instrument or use the built-in struck-bar synth. Deterministic, musical, and always in sync with your DAW.

---

### For Composers / Academics

Focus on **process, heritage, research**.

**Example:**
> Drift is a phase-based generative sequencer rooted in Steve Reich's process music. Two independent playheads drift over a single pattern; the result is audible phasing and interference. Built from DMA-level algorithmic composition research: transparent, deterministic, and grounded in music theory and compositional practice.

---

### For Live Performers

Focus on **reliability, control, capture**.

**Example:**
> Drift gives you evolving patterns that stay musical and in sync. No randomness, no surprises: deterministic logic means the same settings always produce the same output. Capture moments as they happen; every performance is repeatable and controllable.

---

### For Experimenters / IDM / Modular

Focus on **structure, math, niche appeal**.

**Example:**
> Drift uses phase-based sequencing: two playheads at different rates over one loop. Reich-style interference, prime-length loops (optional), and scale quantization. Transparent math; no black box. For when you want structure, not randomness.

## Checklist: Does This Copy Align?

Before publishing any copy, check:

- [ ] Is the **process** or **product type** clearly named? (Not vague "generative")
- [ ] Is it **confident but not loud**? (No "revolutionary," "game-changing")
- [ ] Is it **warm and inviting**? (Not cold/clinical or overly playful)
- [ ] Does it emphasize **control and transparency**? ("You choose…" / "deterministic" / "logic")
- [ ] Does it reference **craft or heritage** if appropriate? (Reich, DMA, process music)
- [ ] Is it **short and clear**? (One idea per sentence; cut filler)
- [ ] Does it **avoid AI hype**? (No "AI-powered," "neural," "intelligent")

If yes to all: ship it.
